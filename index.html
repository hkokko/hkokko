    Passive Blocking / Ward Location

      Placement of weapons and shields so that they act as cover.

    • Characters hold weapon/shield so it covers a chosen Hit Location (or Hit Locations when using a shield). They cannot then actively Parry with it (see Ward Location page 93).
    • Any Attack which hits those Hit Locations reduces damage as normal
    • If Defender has two weapons or weapon and shield combatant can use other weapon to actively Parry
    • Choose Location Special Effect cannot be used to bypass the block
    • Crouching down behind a shield allows a character to double the number of Hit Locations covered whilst Passive Blocking.

    Mythras Core Rules 93,105

    Counterspell

  • Can attempt to dismiss or counter an incoming spell, miracle or spirit.
  • This assumes the countering magic has a casting time of one Turn, otherwise it must be prepared in advance, and temporarily withheld using the Hold Magic action.
  • Intercepting magic negates the entire spell or miracle, even those with multiple targets or areas of effect.
    • Mythras Core Rules 92

    Interrupt

    • (Delaying characters only)
    • Halts an opponent’s turn after his declaration in order to take your own delayed turn.
    • Assuming no change in the tactical situation, the opponent continues his turn after yours is completed.
    • If unable to still achieve his original declaration, the opponent’s Action Point is wasted.
    • An interrupt can also be used against anyone passing close by the delaying character, within Weapon Reach.

    Mythras Core Rules 92

    Heroic Last Action

  • In special cases where key characters receive a Major Wound,
  • the Games Master might allow a heroic last action - such as spitting out one last spell, attempting to cut down their slayer, crawling away to hide, or trying to tourniquet their own wound.
  • They must have working limbs applicable to the action, and burn a Luck Point.
  • They get a single opportunity to attempt their action, and then immedi- ately collapse into unconsciousness, and probable death.

    • Mythras Core Rules 111

    Level of Success

  • One Level of Success (critical vs success, success vs failure) grants one Special Effect
  • Two Levels of Success (critical vs failure, success vs fumble) grants two Special Effects
  • Three Levels of Success (critical vs fumble) grants three Special Effects

    Charging into contact

  • Crash into opponent and stay engaged.
  • From that moment on combat proceeds normally.
  • All benefits gained from the Charge are lost after the first Attack.

    • Mythras Core Rules 102

    Charging through contact

  • Use the momentum to carry the Attacker through, and clear of an engagement.
  • It is normally limited to mounted combatants or creatures of significant size unlikely to be hindered by an impact with smaller foes.
  • The speed of such Charges prevents the Attacker and Defender from exchanging more than a single action with each other,namely the Charge Attack, and the Defender’s reaction to it (Parry, Evade or Counterattack).
  • Charges can be done by running, swimming or flying creatures.

    • Mythras Core Rules 102

    Surprise

  • The target suffers a -10 penalty to Initiative
  • Until their Initiative arrives cannot defend
  • The first Attack on the target, if successful, gains a bonus Special Effect
  • For the remainder of the Round they may not perform any offensive action
  • Targets of such an Attack should be permitted a chance to detect the impending conflict.

    • Mythras Core Rules 105

    Leaping Attacks

      When

    • Most leaping Attacks are triggered as part of an ambush or at the conclusion of a Charge.
    • Some creatures have the ability to leap atop an opponent without the need for a run-up or superior height.
    • Leaping Attacks only work against opponents of up to twice the Attacker’s SIZ.
    • Target can Brace Combat Action, if aware of leap Attack.

      • Leaper’s Athletics vs Defender’s Brawn or Evade

    • Quadruped may substitute Athletics for Evade, making Attacker’s Athletics roll one Difficulty Grade harder.
    • If the leaper wins then the Defender is automatically knocked prone, with the Attacker astride them. A prone victim cannot recover his footing (combat rolls at Formidable) until either his Attacker leaves him, or using Special Effect Arise.
    • If the Attack fails, the Defender has weathered or sidestepped the impact.
    • Select suitable Special Effects as per normal combat.
    • No damage is inflicted as part of the leap (unless they possess the Leaper creature ability page 216)

    Knockback

  • Knockback damage is counted before any reductions.
  • Easy Acrobatics or standard Athletics roll to avoid falling prone.
  • Thrust backwards one metre for each 5 points of damage (or fraction thereof) in excess of their SIZ.
  • Brace Combat Action may affect knockback.
    • Mythras Core Rules 104

    Brace

  • Take a firm chance and lean into the attack
  • Against Knockback or Leaping Attacks SIZ = 1.5x
  • Against Bash Special Effect SIZ = 2x
  • When character moves - brace is lost
    • Mythras Core Rules 91

    Withdraw from Combat

    In your turn use proactive action to:

  • Use Change Range to open range to beyond range
  • Win and use Withdraw Special Effect
  • Win all opponents in Outmaneuvre
  • Mounted withdraw freely from engagement on your turn provided your mount moves faster than the opponent, and the mount itself is not engaged in attacking.
    • Mythras Core Rules 92, 101,104, 106

    Regain Footing

  • Unengaged can regain his footing from being tripped or knocked down.
  • Engaged, must win an opposed test of Brawn or Athletics with the opponent before standing.
    • Mythras Core Rules 92

    Falling

  • Prone fights at Formidable
  • Prone unable to gain footing can be compelled to surrender
  • If unengaged with an opponent, the character can regain his footing from being tripped or knocked down.
  • If engaged, the character must win an Opposed test of Brawn or Athletics with the opponent before standing.
    • Mythras Core Rules 92, 97, 101

    Unarmed

  • Target needs to be within reach of the attacker’s natural weapons
  • Human attacks are size Small, Reach Touch and 1d3 damage
  • Unarmed Prowess Trait raises blocks and parries to Medium
  • Strike or Grapple - must be announced beforehand
  • Unarmed combat can parry a weapon attack
  • Step inside the reach of opponent for Shorter Reach
  • Natural Weapons of creatures / monsters are considered to reach from Touch to their current Reach without suffering from opponents entering within their reach (Shorter Reach rule
    • Mythras Core Rules 105,222

    Evade

    • Avoid missiles - vs Combat style of attacker
    • Thwarting a trap - vs either POT pr skill rating of maker
    • Evading a spell - vs spell caster's score in the casting skill
    • Manoeuvering in combat - vs attacker's evade skill
    • Whatever the result - evader is prone (and suffers Formidable on evade and fighting until up) and needs to Regain Footing 92 next Turn
    • Mounted character can only evade by flinging themselves from the saddle
    Mythras Core Rules 41,103

    Mounted Combat

  • A mounted character may only Evade by flinging themselves clear of the saddle
  • Combat Skills of a rider may not exceed their Ride skill
  • 1D10+10 for the Hit Location of bipedal creatures, provided the target is lower than the rider.
  • Withdraw freely from engagement on his turn provided his mount moves faster than the opponent, and the mount itself is not engaged in attacking.
  • Use Action Point to substitute either his own Combat Style or Ride skill instead of his mount’s, to defend it against Attacks.
  • Combine his SIZ with his mount’s for the purposes of avoiding Knockback, provided he passes an unOpposed Ride roll.
  • When Charging with a braced weapon, substitute his own Damage Bonus for that of his mount. A weapon braced for a Charging Attack cannot be used to Parry. If the weapon impales it must be left in the body of the target, or else break or dismount the rider as the mount continues past.
    • Mythras Core Rules 104
